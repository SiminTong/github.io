Research

Second Master Research Project (Sept. 2021 - Present)

Supervisor: Dr. (Nienke) van der Marel


In this project, we work on disks in the Serpens star-forming region. Unlike disks in other star-forming regions, such as Taurus (Andrews et al. 2013), Lupus (Ansdell et al. 2016), Ophiuchus (Cieza et al. 2019) and Perseus (Tychoniec et al. 2020), which have been investigated in previous studies, disks in Serpens are more crowded and this will have an dynamical impact on the star/disk formation predicted by simulations. In contrast to the crowded Orion star-forming region, where strong UV radiation from O/B-type stars also have significant effects on the star/disk formation, Serpens is an ideal region to distinguish the consequences of the high stellar density from that of the photoevaporation due to the UV radiation on the star/disk formation.

Continuum emission from the disks and CO emission from the outflows observed by the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) are used in our study. The observed visibility is processed by the Common Astronomy Software Applications package (CASA) for the continuum imaging and line imaging, from which, we do further analysis, like computing continuum and 12CO flux density to characterize the basic properties of systems in Serpens. Data collected in these steps will be further employed to do the statistical and systematic study with comparison to disks in other star-forming regions.
First Master Research Project (Sept. 2020 - Present)

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. (Michiel) Hogerheijde, Dr. (Aida) Ahmadi, Prof. Dr. (Serena) Viti


To investigate whether high-mass stars (with a mass larger than 8 solar mass) are formed in the same way as their low-mass counterparts, surrounded by accretion disks, and whether they undergo monolithic collapse or competitive accretion, this project has a nature of exploration. It started with inventory creation on broad topics about massive star formation. We explored the ALMA Science Archive with the assistance of ALminer, a user-friendly toolkit to search for ALMA projects with either specific parameters, like coordinates, or keywords, such as scientific categories and project absracts, and built three invetories: a. projects observing disks around massive stars; b. projects aiming to test massive star formation theories; c. projects related to the outflows/jets from massive stars.

Then, from the disk inventory, we selected one representative targets, observed in several different bands and with different angular resolutions, for a detailed case study. The data reduction is conducted with CASA and futher analysis, including identification of potential molecular species responsible for the spectra lines and spectral modelling, are implemented with CASSIS.
